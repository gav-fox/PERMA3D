June 2026 - Mapping the environment


The most important dataset in PERMA3D is the site survey, which starts in the field. A survey grade 3D topography is the foundation everything is built on.

I am starting with what I have: an iPhone 14, a spirit level, and a printer.

Using a yet to be determined iPhone app (LiDAR/photogrammetry) with printed ground control points (GCPs) I will scan my first test site, my parents garden, to try to create an accurate 3D model of the site.

The main challenge at this stage is getting accuracy without an RTK device, relying on spirit level and handheld measurements to get an accurate X, Y, Z plot for each GCP.

[Photo: GCPs printed and numbered with sharpie]

As soon as I tried to use a tape measure I realised it would be a challenge to keep measurements level across uneven ground. I needed to measure the horizontal distance and vertical difference between points separately.

[Photo: tape measure attempt]

I sourced a cheap laser distance measure from Amazon, around £20, with built in bubble levels and an accuracy of +-1.5mm.

[Photo: Kiprim LD50E]

Here is the makeshift survey rig: the base of a music stand with a wooden jig, a weighted plumb line dropping through the centre to align over the GCP.

[Photo: survey rig]
